BREAKING NEWS

THE UPRISING OF THOSE WHO REFUSE TO CHANGE: PROTESTS AGAINST HUMAN GENETIC ENGINEERING ESCALATE!

Crisis of faith, a two-class society, and blocked career paths – humanity is divided.

Olaf Hanseberger, Berlin – A picture of anger and despair was seen today on the streets of Berlin. Thousands of people marched to protest against the growing normalization and the serious consequences of genetic engineering in humans.

The "Demonstration against genetically modified humans" – as it was officially called – became a powerful cry for justice and ethics. The main demands of the protesters were many-sided and showed the deep uncertainty among the people.

Religious groups were especially active in the protest. For them, the deliberate changing of the human being—whether to improve performance or to change physical appearance—is hard to imagine.

They believe that "A human is a human. Trying to improve them artificially is arrogant," says Klaudia Rosenberg, spokesperson for the faith community. The question of whether genetic engineering and faith can work together was brought up again and again on the protesters' signs. The fear of losing our humanity and our moral values was clearly felt.

Citizens are angry; they demand equal rights and opportunities for all. According to some statements, the pharmaceutical industry and the government want to create a new "two-class society." They claim this is why the prices for gene injections are being made so extremely expensive.

The dissatisfaction is clear. Many people at the demo want to send a message; they want to be heard and demand more information.

Existential fears are also being expressed. Many people are afraid that finding a job could become much harder because companies might prefer genetically modified humans. Even those already affected are speaking out; they say they have already run into difficulties.

They expressed it this way: "Discrimination against unmodified people in the job market is a growing problem. We demand the right to work and equal treatment, regardless of our genetic status."

MEDICINE

Human 2.0 Medicine Page

The new injection works like a miracle: "The International laboratory for Humans" is making significant progress in the field of medicine. After genetically caused diseases have largely been eliminated, the focus is now increasingly shifting towards optimizing human performance.

In addition to increasing the number of mitochondria, the number of erythrocytes is also being raised. Furthermore, hemoglobin has been modified so that it can bind significantly more oxygen. However, this advancement has only been made possible through the latest scientific breakthrough.

These genetic modifications also affect lung volume, which has been greatly increased. As a result, more air can be taken in and processed. The alveoli have also been enlarged, allowing more oxygen to diffuse into the body.

Muscle regeneration has also been significantly improved and appears almost miraculous. In addition, different muscle fiber types (fast-twitch or slow-twitch) can be specifically enhanced, depending on whether the focus is on endurance or strength.

SOCIETY

Discrimination against Humans 2.0

On the internet and increasingly in comment sections, more and more statements from those affected are appearing. They, too, are dissatisfied and want to be heard.

They report unfair treatment and exclusion in everyday life. In normal daily situations, they often receive hateful looks and are excluded from social circles because they are seen as "unnatural."

By now, there is a term that describes this well: they call it "genetic racism." Some of those affected report that they are "under pressure to function perfectly 24/7."

Mark Van Korn, who is affected himself, describes the following everyday situation: "In completely normal situations, I am constantly excluded. People sometimes insult me and throw words like 'lab-rat' at me. In gym class, some students don't even want people like me around because they think it’s unfair since we are 'unnaturally superior.' It’s simply annoying, and something has to change."
