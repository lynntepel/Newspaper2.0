MEDICINE

Human 2.0 Medicine

The new injection works like a miracle: “The International Laboratory for Humans” is making significant progress in the field of medicine. After genetically caused diseases have largely been eliminated, the focus is now increasingly shifting towards optimizing human performance.

In addition to increasing the number of mitochondria, the number of erythrocytes is also being raised. Furthermore, hemoglobin has been modified so that it can bind significantly more oxygen. However, this advancement has only been made possible through the latest scientific breakthrough.

These genetic modifications also affect lung volume, which has been greatly increased. As a result, more air can be taken in and processed. The alveoli have also been enlarged, allowing more oxygen to diffuse into the body.

Muscle regeneration has also been significantly improved and appears almost miraculous. In addition, different muscle fiber types can be specifically enhanced, depending on whether the focus is on endurance or strength.

BREAKING NEWS

The Uprising of Those Who Refuse to Change

Olaf Hanseberger, Berlin – A picture of anger and despair was seen today on the streets of Berlin. Thousands of people marched to protest against the growing normalization and the serious consequences of genetic engineering in humans.

Religious groups were especially active in the protest. For them, the deliberate changing of the human being — whether to improve performance or to change physical appearance — is hard to imagine. They believe that a human is a human, and trying to improve them artificially is arrogant.

Citizens are demanding equal rights and opportunities for all. Many fear that the pharmaceutical industry and the government want to create a new two-class society by making gene injections extremely expensive.

Existential fears are also being expressed. Many people are afraid that finding a job could become much harder because companies might prefer genetically modified humans.

SOCIETY

Discrimination Against Humans 2.0

On the internet and increasingly in comment sections, more and more statements from those affected are appearing. They report unfair treatment and exclusion in everyday life. In normal daily situations, they often receive hateful looks and are excluded from social circles because they are seen as unnatural.

By now, there is a term that describes this well: “genetic racism.” Some of those affected report that they are under pressure to function perfectly 24/7.

Mark Van Korn, who is affected himself, describes the following everyday situation: “In completely normal situations, I am constantly excluded. People sometimes insult me and throw words like ‘lab-rat’ at me. In gym class, some students do not even want people like me around because they think it is unfair since we are unnaturally superior.”
